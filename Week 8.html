Week 8 Meaning in Interactive Media

Select four significant points made by Turkle (2011) in the weekly reading. Choose quotes from the reading that summarises each point. Find, or make, an image (photo, drawing, diagram) that depicts a particular human-machine interaction that encapsulates each point.


Sherry Turkle made a point that replacing humans with robots for certain jobs such as caregiving the elderly and disadvantaged patients provide practical help and companionship for people in such situation.

  • "We know that we warm to machines when they seem to show interest in us, when their affordances speak to our vulnerabilities"

  • “Some of the aides and nurses at the rehab center hurt you because they are unskilled, and some hurt you because they mean to… One of them, she pulled me by the hair. One dragged me by my tubes. A robot would never do that”

Source "http://www.mspi.se/design-thinking-final-presentation"

The second point that Sherry Turkle make is the authenticity of emotions as developers write and program such emotions into the robot and evoke such written emotions with an algorithm. We know what emotions robots feel and can’t feel.

  • "we animate robotic creatures by projecting meaning onto them and are thus tempted to speak of their emotions and even their 'authenticity'."

  • “What does the robot feel? We know what the robot cannot feel: it cannot feel human empathy or the flow of human connection.”

Source "https://www.madeinvilnius.lt/verslas/technologijos/vilniu-atvyksta-zymiausia-humanoide-pasaulyje-sofija/"

The third point that Sherry Turkle makes is that technology has enveloped our lives such example is the web with creating online acquaintance which many have become emotionally dependant upon.

  • “we are emotionally dependent on online friends and intrigued by robots that, their designers claim, are almost ready to love us.”

  • “One might assume that what we want… informal networks that underpin online acquaintanceship. But if we pay attention to the real consequences of what we think we want, we may discover what we really want. We may want some stillness and solitude”

  • "Every new technology challenges us, generation after generation, to ask whether it serves our human purposes."

Source "http://www.mspi.se/design-thinking-final-presentation"

Sherry Turkle forth point is that generalising technology as an addictive substance is a poor choice/idea. The key point is that it is imperative to be open towards facing problems and humility.

  • “we have to find a way to live with seductive technology and make it work for our purposes.”

  • “It encourages humility, a state of mind in which we are most open to facing problems and reconsidering decisions.”

  • “suggests that we step back and reassess when we hear triumphalist or apocalyptic narratives about how to live with technology.”

Source "https://www.investors.com/news/technology/ai-in-business-future-of-artificial-intelligence/"
