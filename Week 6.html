Week 6 Situated Action

Source "http://www.mspi.se/design-thinking-final-presentation"

Analyse the way the creative mind is extended by its context. Select an image of a creative task in progress (e.g. a person doing craft work, art or design) and unpack how the tools and environment are used to support the task. Speculate on other (not visible in the image) external influences that might also be supporting the task.


Prototyping is a key process in developing design and products. Prototypes are commonly created with simple and general materials that can be found in arts and craft stores. Examples of cost effective materials.

  • Cardboard
  • Popsicle stick
  • Sticky notes
  • Clay
  • Lego

Cardboards are have many positive features such as light weight, cheap, foldable, rigid and impact resistense. As for popsicle sticks they are sturdy and cheap which is often used to create miniture house models. Which are very appealing features for most beginning prototypes. Sticky notes are very useful as it can be writen or drawn on and stick onto a wall or board. Such material is also used in the image above.

Tools are also very important for designing prototypes as they help shape them. These tools are can often be found in common households. As for most of the things used to create prototypes. Such examples.

  • Scissors
  • Sticky Tape
  • Razor Cutter
  • Glue Gun
  • Glue
  • Ruler

Scissors are by far the most common tool used to create prototypes to cut paper or cardboard to shape. As for sticky tape it can quickly and easily attach pieces or parts of the prototypes together with great integrity. Which can also be taken apart with little effort and for razor cutter it can cut cardboard clean and straight with ease. It can be assumed from the image that the enironment the picture was taken from is an office.
